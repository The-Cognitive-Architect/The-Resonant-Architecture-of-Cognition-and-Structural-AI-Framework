The Three Pillars of the Architecture

Ontologically Modulated Executive Function (OMEF)

A non-volitional gating mechanism. Cognitive engagement isn't a choice; it's an automatic response to tasks that align with an individual's core sense of meaning and coherence.

The gate only opens for resonant tasks, leaving the vast majority of conventional demands unaddressed.

False-Structure Intolerance (FSI)

An ontological immune system. It triggers an involuntary, full-system shutdown in response to arbitrary rules, incoherent logic, or inauthentic demands to protect systemic integrity.

This "somatic veto" is a protective mechanism, not defiance. It ruthlessly filters out ontological noise.

State-Contingent Motivational Filtering (SCMF)

An energy regulation system. It dictates an oscillatory rhythm of intense, high-bandwidth engagement ("meaning storms") followed by periods of mandatory cognitive reset and recovery.

This cycle is not a flaw but a bio-cognitive efficiency mechanism, optimizing for deep, meaningful work.
